All About the NYC Community Activist Ryan Carson 

Ryan Carson (age 32, 1991 – October 2, 2023) was a New York based social justice activist whose life was cut short by a violent individual wielding a knife in the early hours of October 2, 2023.

He and his girlfriend Claudia Morales were waiting for a bus at a stop when an assailant, initially unrecognizable and identified as a hobo, launched a brutal knife attack on him.

Shockingly, Ryan was stabbed multiple times in the chest, as captured by a CCTV camera at the scene.

Emergency services rushed him to a nearby hospital, but the injuries proved fatal, leading to his untimely passing. Law enforcement officials are actively investigating to apprehend the assailant.

Carson, a passionate advocate for social justice, had a mission to improve the state of New York. Amid this tragedy, a GoFundMe campaign has been initiated to gather funds for Ryan’s memorial and funeral expenses, allowing the community to unite and honor his memory.

Ryan Carson’s Career

Ryan was a remarkably gifted individual with a distinctive perspective on the world. Upon completing his college education, he joined NYPIRG in 2017 as a Regional Supervisor, overseeing the hiring, training, and supervision of NYPIRG officers.

For nearly four years, he excelled in this role before transitioning to the position of Senior Solid Waste Campaign Manager. In this new capacity, Ryan passionately advocated for enhancing the Bigger Better Bottle Bill to increase recycling rates.

He argued that the existing five-cent return was insufficient for contemporary times, asserting that a ten-cent deposit would encourage a higher percentage of recycled items, especially in climate change concerns, with New York’s recycling rate at approximately 64% as of 2020.

Beyond his professional endeavors, Ryan was widely recognized as a prominent activist. In 2021, deeply affected by the loss of a close friend to an overdose, he embarked on an impactful journey, walking over 150 miles from New York City to Albany.

This journey was dedicated to raising awareness about overdose prevention centers and harm reduction resources. Colleagues and friends attested to Ryan’s significant contributions to society, considering him a valuable asset to the community.
