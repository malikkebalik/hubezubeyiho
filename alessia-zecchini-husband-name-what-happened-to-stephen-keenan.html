What Happened to Stephen Keenan?

Stephen Keenan was an Irish freediving instructor and co-founder of the Dahab Freedivers school and center in Egypt. He was a beloved member of the diving community, known for risking his life to save other divers in distress. Tragically, in July 2017, during a dive gone wrong in the Blue Hole, a popular diving spot off the coast of Dahab, Egypt, Keenan lost his life while attempting to rescue Alessia Zecchini.

How Did Stephen Keenan Die?

During the ill-fated dive, Zecchini found herself swimming in the wrong direction, facing potential danger. Stephen Keenan, her safety diver, bravely left the safety of the guiding rope to aid her. The last photo of Keenan alive shows him pushing Zecchini ahead of himself towards the surface. Both divers experienced a “blackout” as they resurfaced, but Keenan was found face down and unconscious. He lost his life in the valiant act of saving Zecchini.

The documentary features an emotional interview with Zecchini, where she reflects on the devastating dive that led to Keenan’s death.
